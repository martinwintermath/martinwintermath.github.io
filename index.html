Martin Winter, PhD
winter@math.tu-berlin.de

I am a mathematician. I am Dirichlet fellow at TU Berlin and member of the Nonlinear Algebra group at the Max-Planck-Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences in Leipzig. I am principal investigator in the priority program “Combinatorial Synergies”.

My research is in discrete geometry, real algebraic geometry, geometric rigidity, semi-definite optimization, spectral graph theory, algebraic combinatorics and low-dimensional topology. Most recently I am studying the Wachspress Geometry of convex polytopes. I am also working on the formalization of polyhedral geometry in Lean (see the work in progress on GitHub).

Until 2024 I was postdoc at the University of Warwick (UK). I got my PhD from TU Chemnitz (Germany) in 2021.
