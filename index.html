Martin Winter, PhD
winter@math.tu-berlin.de

I am a mathematician. I am Dirichlet fellow at TU Berlin and guest researcher at the Max-Planck-Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences in Leipzig. I am researching in discrete geometry, real algebraic geometry, geometic constraint systems, semi-definite optimization, spectral graph theory, algebraic combinatorics and low-dimensional topology. Most recently I am studying the Wachspress Geometry of convex polytopes.

I am principal investigator in the priority program “Combinatorial Synergies” and my project is “Wachspress Coordinates - a Bridge between Algebra, Geometry and Combinatorics”.

Before, I was postdoc at the University of Warwick (UK). I got my PhD from TU Chemnitz (Germany) in 2021.
